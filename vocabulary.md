## Words & Phrases

| # | Word / Phrase | Type | CEFR | Meaning | Synonyms | Example Sentence |
|---|---------------|------|------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **competency** | noun | B2 | the ability to do something successfully or efficiently | capability, ability, skill, aptitude | "The job listing requires competency in Python and cloud infrastructure." |
| 2 | **proficiency** | noun | C1 | a high degree of skill or expertise, especially from practice | mastery, fluency, competence, skillfulness | "She demonstrated proficiency in machine learning by deploying the model in under a day." |
| 3 | **technical expertise** | noun phrase | C1 | deep, specialized knowledge in a technical field | domain knowledge, specialist knowledge, technical mastery | "The team hired a contractor with technical expertise in distributed systems." |
| 4 | **it seems to me** | phrase | B2 | used to introduce a personal opinion or observation | in my opinion, from my perspective, I believe, as far as I can tell | "It seems to me that this architecture will create a bottleneck at scale." |
| 5 | **scalability** | noun | C1 | the ability of a system to handle growing amounts of work or be enlarged to accommodate growth | elasticity, expandability, growth capacity | "The team chose Kubernetes because of its scalability — it can handle thousands of containers as traffic grows." |
| 6 | **prioritise** | verb | B2 | to decide which tasks or problems are most important and deal with them first | rank, order, focus on, put first | "We need to prioritise fixing the security vulnerability before shipping the new feature." |
| 7 | **manage to** | phrasal verb | B2 | to succeed in doing something, especially something difficult | succeed in, accomplish, achieve, pull off | "After two days of debugging, she managed to track down the memory leak." |
| 8 | **make it** | phrase | B2 | to succeed in reaching a goal or position; to attend something | succeed, get there, pull through | "Not everyone who starts a bootcamp makes it to the final project — it requires real dedication." |
| 9 | **mastering (a skill)** | verb (gerund) | C1 | achieving a very high level of skill or understanding in something through sustained effort | learning thoroughly, becoming fluent in, conquering | "Mastering programming languages takes years of consistent practice and real-world projects." |
| 10 | **centralize** | verb | B2 | to bring things under a single central system or point of control | consolidate, unify, concentrate, bring together | "We decided to centralize all our logging data in one platform to make debugging easier." |
| 11 | **consolidation** | noun | C1 | the process of combining several things into a single more effective whole | merging, unification, integration, streamlining | "The consolidation of our microservices into a shared library reduced code duplication significantly." |
| 12 | **eligible** | adjective | B2 | meeting the required conditions to do or receive something | qualified, suitable, entitled, permitted | "Only engineers who have completed the security training are eligible to access the production environment." |
| 13 | **place a strong emphasis on** | phrase | C1 | to give particular importance or attention to something | prioritise, focus heavily on, stress, highlight | "Our engineering culture places a strong emphasis on code reviews and knowledge sharing." |
| 14 | **collaborative working environment** | noun phrase | C1 | a workplace where people actively work together, share knowledge, and support each other | team-oriented culture, cooperative setting | "A collaborative working environment helps junior developers grow faster through peer learning." |
| 15 | **professional advancement** | noun phrase | C1 | progress and growth in one's career, skills, or status | career growth, career progression, upward mobility | "He joined the company because it offered clear paths for professional advancement in the AI field." |
| 16 | **extensive collaboration** | noun phrase | C1 | working closely and frequently with a wide range of people or teams | broad teamwork, cross-team cooperation | "The project required extensive collaboration between the frontend, backend, and DevOps teams." |
| 17 | **coordinate with team members** | phrase | B2 | to organise and align efforts with colleagues to work effectively together | sync with, align with, work alongside | "Before starting the sprint, make sure to coordinate with team members on task dependencies." |
| 18 | **well-rounded software engineer** | noun phrase | C1 | a developer with a broad mix of technical and soft skills across multiple areas | versatile engineer, full-spectrum developer | "Being a well-rounded software engineer means you can write code, communicate clearly, and understand business needs." |
| 19 | **stakeholder** | noun | C1 | a person or group with an interest in or affected by a project or business decision | interested party, decision-maker, sponsor, client | "We scheduled a demo to keep stakeholders informed about the progress of the new AI feature." |
| 20 | **iterate** | verb | B2 | to repeat a process, making improvements each time based on feedback or results | refine, improve progressively, cycle through | "We iterate on the design based on user feedback until the experience feels right." |
| 21 | **bottleneck** | noun | C1 | a point in a system where flow is restricted, causing slowdowns for the whole process | choke point, constraint, limiting factor | "The database query is a bottleneck slowing down the entire pipeline." |
| 22 | **leverage** | verb | C1 | to use something to its maximum advantage, especially an existing resource or tool | utilise, capitalise on, take advantage of | "We can leverage the existing API instead of building the integration from scratch." |
| 23 | **trade-off** | noun | C1 | a balance between two desirable but competing things where gaining one means losing some of the other | compromise, balancing act, give-and-take | "There's a trade-off between performance and readability here — we need to decide what matters more." |
| 24 | **hands-on experience** | noun phrase | B2 | practical experience gained by directly doing something rather than just studying it | practical experience, real-world experience, applied experience | "She has hands-on experience deploying ML models in production environments." |
| 25 | **take ownership of** | phrase | C1 | to accept full responsibility for something and see it through from start to finish | be accountable for, own, be responsible for | "Each engineer is expected to take ownership of their services end-to-end." |
| 26 | **proof of concept** | noun phrase | C1 | a small-scale test or prototype to demonstrate that an idea or approach is feasible | prototype, pilot, feasibility test, PoC | "Let's build a proof of concept before committing to this architecture." |
| 27 | **align with** | phrasal verb | B2 | to be consistent with or match something, such as a goal, strategy, or standard | match, fit, be consistent with, support | "This solution aligns with our long-term technical roadmap." |
| 28 | **cross-functional** | adjective | C1 | involving people with different specialisations working together toward a shared goal | multidisciplinary, diverse, cross-team | "A cross-functional team includes developers, designers, and product managers." |
| 29 | **scalable solution** | noun phrase | C1 | an approach or system that can grow and handle increased demand without breaking down | flexible architecture, growth-ready system | "We need a scalable solution that can support 10x growth in traffic." |
| 30 | **under the hood** | phrase | C1 | referring to the internal workings of a system, hidden from the end user | internally, behind the scenes, at the technical level | "Under the hood, the model uses a transformer architecture to process language." |
